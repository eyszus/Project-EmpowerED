Project EmpowerED

A data-driven look at literacy outcomes across the Philippines.

This project investigates the state of reading proficiency among Filipino students and the factors that may influence literacy outcomes in public schools. Literacy is a key foundation for quality education and long-term national development, yet international assessments have consistently shown that Filipino students struggle in reading comprehension compared to many other countries.

Through a data-driven analysis, our project examines how socioeconomic status and geographic location relate to reading proficiency outcomes. By analyzing datasets from international and national education studies, we aim to identify patterns that can help explain disparities in literacy performance across the Philippines.

Photo: Philstar

The Problem

Recent international education assessments, particularly the Programme for International Student Assessment (PISA), have placed the Philippines among the lowest performing countries in reading comprehension. This raises important questions about the underlying factors that shape literacy outcomes among Filipino students.

Educational inequality may stem from differences in socioeconomic status, parental education, and geographic location. Students from low-income households or rural communities may face limited access to learning resources, fewer educational opportunities, and structural disadvantages that affect literacy development.

Research Questions

  • How does the reading proficiency distribution of Filipino students in School Year 2024–2025 compare to the latest Southeast Asian benchmarks from PISA?
  • To what degree do household income and parental education correlate with standardized reading comprehension scores in the Philippines?
  • Is there a statistically significant difference in literacy outcomes between students from urban and rural public elementary schools?

Hypotheses

  • Filipino students will have lower reading proficiency compared to the latest Southeast Asian benchmarks from PISA.
  • Lower household income and parental education will be associated with lower reading comprehension scores.
  • Students from rural public schools will demonstrate significantly lower literacy outcomes than students from urban schools.

Approach

To investigate these questions, we analyze multiple datasets related to education and socioeconomic indicators. These include international large-scale assessments and national statistics datasets that provide information about literacy performance, household income, parental education, and geographic classification of schools.

Statistical analysis will be used to measure correlations between socioeconomic indicators and reading scores, while comparative analysis will evaluate differences between urban and rural student populations.

Data Sources

  • PISA 2022 Database
  • SEA-PLM 2024 Dataset
  • PSA FLEMMS 2024
